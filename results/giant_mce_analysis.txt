Giant-MCE (Gd5Si2Ge2, first-order Landau model) vs. Gd, at their
own favorable operating points vs. the ASHRAE 18-27C (291-300K) range
==========================================================================================
Gd peak-effect temperature: 296.5 K (23.4 C) -- INSIDE the ASHRAE recommended supply range
Gd5Si2Ge2 peak-effect temperature: 286.4 K (13.2 C) -- BELOW the ASHRAE range by ~4.6 K

Test 1: BOTH materials at the ASHRAE operating point (T_cold=291K, span=10.0K)
  Gd:         Qc=  1241.5 W   COP_elec=7.42
  Gd5Si2Ge2:  Qc=     0.0 W   COP_elec=0.00   <- collapses to ~0: 10.0K span is centered ~9.6K from its own peak

Test 2: Gd5Si2Ge2 at ITS OWN favorable point (T_cold=281.4K = 8.2C, span=10.0K, straddling its 286.4K peak)
  Gd5Si2Ge2:  Qc=  5319.3 W   COP_elec=7.76   <- strong performance when correctly targeted
  Gd (same point): Qc=     0.0 W   COP_elec=0.00   <- Gd fails here (point is far from Gd's own 296.5K)

CONCLUSION:
The giant-MCE effect is real and large when the material is operated within
its own narrow first-order transition window -- but Gd5Si2Ge2's window sits
~5K below the ASHRAE data-center range, so it is not directly
usable for this application as-is. This does NOT overturn the Phase 1-4
conclusion (Gd trails vapor-compression/liquid cooling on COP within the
ASHRAE range). What it DOES support: literature confirms the Gd5(SixGe1-x)4
family has composition-tunable ordering temperature (Pecharsky & Gschneidner,
Appl. Phys. Lett. 70, 3299 (1997), report tunability from ~20K to ~276K by
Si:Ge ratio, with Gd5Si4 itself ordering at 335K) -- so a composition between
Gd5Si2Ge2 and Gd5Si4 tuned to ~291-300K, IF it retains first-order/giant
character at that composition, is the genuinely promising untested direction
for closing the COP gap. This is a materials-synthesis question outside what
a simulation suite alone can answer -- flagged as Phase 6 in ROADMAP.md, not
claimed as solved here.

For reference, baselines at this operating point: VCC COP=12.22, Liquid COP=19.89

Note also: even correctly targeted, Gd5Si2Ge2's COP_electrical (7.76) is close to Gd's own COP_electrical at its matched point (7.42), not dramatically higher, despite ~4x the cooling capacity (Qc). This is consistent with Phase 3's Sobol finding: COP_electrical is driven mainly by frequency/flow/field-dependent losses (loss_model.py), not by which material is loaded into the regenerator -- a bigger MCE mostly buys more Qc per kg, not a better COP.
